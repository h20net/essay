How to Write an Essay

Upper Secondary

Step 1: read the assignment

Decide what kind of essay it is - opinion OR for and against

Step 2: underline key words

Underline key vocabulary in the question and write words with the same or related
meaning (synonyms) or use a couple of words to express the same (rephrase). This
will really save you a lot of time later on. It will also help you avoid repeating words.

Step 3: get ideas

Think of arguments and reasons or examples to support your ideas.
Think of counterarguments and reasons or examples to support your ideas.
Give your opinion in the conclusion

Step 4: make a layout

Write a plan for your essay, write down key words, you will use fourparagraphs:

1. Introduction: 3 sentences
2. Two arguments + reasons or examples: 7 sentences
3. Two counterarguments + reasons or examples: 7 sentences
4. Conclusion: 3 sentences
This will give you 20 sentences. An average of 10 words each, that is 200 words.

Step 5: write

Use your plan (steps 3 and 4) and the vocabulary from step 1 to write your essay.
Remember to use phrases for presenting arguments (p.19) and linking words (p. 20)
